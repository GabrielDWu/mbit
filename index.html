mBIT

Montgomery Blair Informatics Tournament

Overview

mBIT is a competitive programming competition for middle and high school students of all skill levels, organized by students from Montgomery Blair High School. The competition will take place on Saturday, November 2, 2019.

Students will work in teams of up to four to solve interesting and challenging programming problems. There will be prizes and certificates for the top teams in each division.

For more information, check out the logistics and rules pages. If you have any questions, feel free to email us at mbit.organizers@gmail.com.

Sample Problems

These sample problems are provided to give you an idea of which divison you should register for; if you can solve each of the rookie ones easily, you should compete under varsity.

Rookie:

  • While loops
  • Recursion

Varsity:

  • Dynamic Programming
  • Segment Trees

If you are looking to practice for the competition, old USACO problems and the USACO training pages are great places to start.
