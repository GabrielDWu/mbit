Overview

mBIT is a competitive programming competition for middle and high school students of all skill levels, organized by students from Montgomery Blair High School. The competition will take place on Saturday, November 2, 2019 from 9:00 AM to 2:30 PM.

Students will work in teams of up to four to solve interesting and challenging programming problems. There will be prizes for the top teams in each division.

For more information, be sure to visit the logistics and rules pages. If you have any questions about the competition, feel free to email us at mbit.organizers@gmail.com.

Sample Problems

These sample problems are provided to give you an idea of which divison you should register for; if you can solve each of the rookie ones easily, you should probably compete under varsity. Keep in mind that the problems in the competition will primarily focus on thinking and problem solving, not rote memorization of algorithms and data structures.

The sample problems are available for download here, and the solutions are available here (but you should try them first!).

If you are looking for more practice for the competition, old USACO problems and the USACO training pages are great places to start.
