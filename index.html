mBIT

Montgomery Blair Informatics Tournament

Overview

mBIT is a competitive programming competition for middle and high school students of all skill levels organized by students from Montgomery Blair High School.

Students work in teams of four to solve interesting and challenging programming problems.

For more information, check out the logistics and rules pages. If you have any questions, email us at mbit.organizers@gmail.com.

Divisions

mBIT has two divisions: rookie and varsity.

Rookie:

  • Problems range from basic program structure (for, while, if), to string manipulation, to programming concepts such as recursion.
  • Meant for students who have never participated in a competitive programming competition before.

Varsity:

  • Problems range from concepts such as recursion, to dynamic programming, to segment trees and beyond.
  • Meant for students who have experience with competitive programming (i.e. USACO Silver or above, PClassic, etc.)

For information on what division to choose, read the rules page and sample problems.

Sample Problems

These sample problems are provided to give you an idea of what divison you should register for; if you can solve each of the rookie ones within a couple minutes, you should probably compete under varsity.
